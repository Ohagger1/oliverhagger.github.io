Quesitons and Conclusions
  1. Does Asia, having the highest IQ per continent, correlate with the number of students per teacher?
    • It is unclear whether this correlation exists. In other words, it is not known whether the high IQ scores in Asia can be attributed to a lower student-to-teacher ratio, where teachers are able to give more individualized attention to each student. Further research is needed to determine if such a correlation exists.
  2. Europe have an above-average student-to-teacher ratio compared to the rest of the world?
    • Yes, all countries in Europe fall below the worldwide average of 23.5 students to one teacher.
  3. Does Africa have the highest student-to-teacher ratio among all continents?
    • Yes, Africa has the highest average student-to-teacher ratio compared to other continents.

Quesitons and Conclusions
  1. In the 1960s, the Cold War was raging on. But now, who has had more successful or unsuccessful space launch attempts?
    • The USSR has had the highest number of launch attempts, with the majority of them coming from one company.
  2. Is the current Cold War between three countries instead of the two from previous eras?
    • The USSR and USA have significantly more launch attempts compared to China.
  3. Does NASA have the majority of launch attempts in the USA?
    • No, General Dynamics has the highest number of attempts. However, this company launches satellites rather than conventional space rockets.

Quesitons and Conclusions
  1. Do casual or member users have longer rides?
    • Casual users by far have a longer ride length.
  2. Is the total ride length longer on weekends or during the week?
    • The total ride length increases slowly from Monday to Friday, but then drops off from Friday to Saturday. The average ride length is longer on weekdays compared to weekends.
  3. Does ride length for casual and members differ from weekdays to weekends?
    • The ride length increases for member users on weekends, while for casual users it decreases on weekends.
